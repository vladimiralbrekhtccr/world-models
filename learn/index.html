Learn 3DGS — interactive companion

A hands-on partner to LEARN_3DGS.md. Two widgets: one Gaussian you can manipulate, and a real scene made of millions of them.

1 · Anatomy of one Gaussian

This is the primitive — the "atom" of a 3DGS scene. Every number below is literally stored, per Gaussian, in the file. A scene is millions of these overlapping.

Try this: drag scale Z down to near zero — the ellipsoid becomes a flat disc, the "splat" that tiles a wall. That is anisotropy: each Gaussian stretches independently to fit surfaces. Then drop opacity — see how it turns into a soft, see-through blob rather than a hard pebble. That softness is why they blend.

2 · ...and a scene is millions of them

This is the WorldMirror "Statue Face" scene — about 2.3 million of the ellipsoid above, each with its own position, shape, opacity and colour, projected and blended in real time. No mesh, no neural network running — just project, sort, blend.
